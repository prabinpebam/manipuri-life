Tech Stack Research & Decision

A deep survey of the engines, libraries, and tools we could build this game with - what's free vs paid, the pros and cons of each, what we must build ourselves versus reuse, the best-fit stack combinations, and a scored recommendation.

TL;DR

For a cozy, data-driven 2D pixel-art life-sim that must ship on PC, mobile, and console, the field narrows to five serious engines: Godot 4, Unity, GameMaker, MonoGame, and Defold. On a weighted matrix, Godot 4 leads (best 2D fit, zero cost/royalties, strong tooling), with MonoGame and Unity tied as fallbacks. Around the engine, almost everything we need is free and open-source - Aseprite, Tiled/LDtk, Yarn Spinner/Ink, SQLite, Git LFS, GitHub Actions. The main real costs are console porting (the big one), optionally FMOD for adaptive audio above budget thresholds, and small one-offs. The things we genuinely build from scratch are the historical fact-to-content pipeline, the game-specific simulation (calendar, relationships, economy, quests), and the Manipuri content itself.

Status: research & proposal, not a locked decision

Per How We Work, the engine choice is deliberately open and best settled by building small tech POCs in the top candidates. This page is the research that frames those POCs and a recommendation to test against - not a final commitment. The pricing below was web-researched and is current as of mid-2026 (see Sources); vendor terms change often, so re-verify before any purchase.

What we're building (the research context)

The tech has to serve a very specific product. These are the requirements that actually drive the choices below - a stack is "good" only insofar as it serves them.

Genre & feel
A cozy, low-stakes 2D pixel-art, isometric life-sim RPG - quest- and relationship-led, calm, no twitch precision. See the proposal and art explorations.
Platforms
PC + mobile + console as one product, with an input-agnostic, mobile-first design and the lowest-end target phone setting the performance ceiling (see Cross-Platform Strategy).
Content model
Data-driven: crops, NPCs, items, festivals, and events are generated or validated against the structured Historical Database. The pipeline is the technical spine, not the renderer.
Systems
Time/season calendar, day/night cycle, relationships, dialogue-heavy quests, light farming/economy, collections, and save/progression across long arcs.
Team & economics
A small passion-project team, budget-conscious, favouring free/open-source and no-royalty tools, with longevity and low licensing risk valued highly.
Scope guards
Single-player, offline-first; no heavy multiplayer, 3D, or live-service back-end in v1 - which removes whole categories of cost and complexity.

The engine - the one decision everything hangs on

The engine sets the language, the tooling, the 2D pipeline, and - critically - how console shipping works. Five candidates are genuinely viable for this game; the rest are ruled out for missing console paths, weak 2D, or abandonment risk.

EngineLicense & costProsCons
Godot 4
Free / OSS		 MIT, fully free, no royalties, no seat fee, no revenue threshold. Console via paid third-party W4 Consoles (from ~US$2,000/yr for studios under US$300k revenue, ~US$10,000/yr above). Best-in-class 2D; tiny footprint; GDScript and C#; superb tilemap/scene tools; fastest-growing engine; you own the whole stack (source access); ideal for data-driven design (Resources). No official console export - needs a paid porting partner; C#-on-console still maturing; smaller asset ecosystem than Unity.
Unity 6
Free tier + paid		 Free "Personal" under US$200k revenue/funding (raised from $100k); then Unity Pro US$2,200/seat/yr (from Jan 2025). Runtime Fee cancelled (Sep 2024). Most mature; official console support via platform partners; huge Asset Store; C#; massive hiring pool and tutorials. Cost at scale; trust/licensing volatility after the 2023 Runtime-Fee saga (community is actively migrating away); heavier and more 3D-centric than a 2D cozy game needs.
GameMaker
Free + paid tiers		 Free (non-commercial); Professional US$99.99 one-time (commercial, no console); console needs Enterprise US$79.99/mo (~US$960/yr) + registered platform dev. Purpose-built for 2D with 25 years of tooling and a built-in sprite editor; proven hits (Undertale, Hyper Light Drifter, Katana ZERO); official, integrated console exports; fastest path to a playable prototype. Proprietary GML language; closed source; less general-purpose; weaker for a large data-driven content pipeline; single-vendor lock-in.
MonoGame
Free / OSS		 MIT, free. Console via registered dev accounts + NDA console SDKs (community/consulting support). Proven cozy pedigree - Stardew Valley and Celeste are built on XNA/MonoGame; C#; total control; no royalties; extremely stable and long-lived. It's a framework, not an engine - no editor; you build tooling, pipeline, and scene systems yourself. More upfront engineering.
Defold
Free		 Free, no fees/royalties (source-available license). Console via publisher partners. Tiny builds; genuinely cross-platform incl. mobile; strong 2D; managed by a stable steward (originally King). Lua; smaller community; console path depends on partners; fewer ready-made systems for a deep RPG.

Ruled out (for this game)

LÖVE / Phaser / Solar2D - great for prototyping, but no first-class console path. Bevy (Rust) - exciting and free, but young, editor-less, and no console story yet. Unreal - overkill for 2D pixel-art and royalty-bearing. Cocos - mobile-strong but weaker fit for a cozy desktop-and-console RPG.

What the community says (forums & shipped games)

Beyond spec sheets, it's worth listening to what developers on Reddit, Steam forums, and gamedev blogs actually recommend - and what the adoption data shows. The signal is remarkably consistent.

trending_up

Momentum is with Godot

At the GMTK Game Jam 2025 (9,724 entries) Godot held 39% of entries vs GameMaker's ~5% - up from 13% four years earlier (it tripled). On Steam 2024 releases, Unity leads ~51%, with Godot ~5% and GameMaker ~4%; Godot's growth is largely at Unity's expense. r/godot (~307k) now dwarfs r/gamemaker (~94k).

forum

The common forum verdict

A recurring ranking in 2D threads is "for 2D: Godot > GameMaker > Defold." GameMaker is praised as the fastest zero-to-prototype with the cheapest integrated console path (~US$80/mo); Godot is favoured for zero lifetime cost, source access, and 2D-first design. Unity's 2023 Runtime-Fee episode still drives migration and distrust.

workspace_premium

Both have shipped hits with tiny teams

MonoGame: Stardew Valley (one developer) and Celeste. GameMaker: Undertale, Hyper Light Drifter, Katana ZERO (~$32M). Godot: Brotato (10M+ copies), Buckshot Roulette (8M), Dome Keeper. The engine won't cap our commercial ceiling.

swap_horiz

Notable real-world switches

Blobfish moved GameMaker → Godot for Brotato; Buckshot Roulette's dev moved Unity → Godot and cited "not a lot of friction experimenting." For our Stardew-like specifically, cozy-game threads most often land on Godot or GameMaker, with MonoGame recommended to those who want the literal Stardew/Celeste path.

Read the sentiment with a grain of salt

Forums skew toward solo/hobbyist PC devs, who undervalue console economics - where GameMaker's integrated path or Unity's turnkey support genuinely lead. And popularity isn't suitability: our decision still turns on our weighted criteria and the POCs, not on vote counts. Figures and quotes above are cited in Sources.

Build from scratch vs reuse

The winning strategy is to buy/borrow the generic plumbing and spend our scarce engineering on what makes this game unique. A clear split:

Reuse (off-the-shelf)

  • Engine, renderer, physics, input, audio playback
  • Pixel-art & animation tools (Aseprite), tilemap editor (Tiled/LDtk)
  • Dialogue runtime (Yarn Spinner / Ink)
  • SQLite, serialization formats, localization framework
  • Git/LFS, CI, crash/analytics (privacy-respecting, optional)
  • Audio middleware (FMOD) only if adaptive music demands it

Build ourselves

  • The fact-to-content pipeline: schema, validation, exporter
  • Game simulation: calendar/seasons, day/night, weather
  • Relationship, quest, and economy systems from history
  • Collections/almanac and the "learn by playing" surfacing
  • Palette & day/night shaders; isometric Y-sort niceties
  • Save migration; the bespoke authoring tool glue
  • All the Manipuri content itself (the real work)

How to read it

Godot 4 wins on 2D fit, cost, and control; its one weakness is console, which is a solvable, paid problem, not a wall. MonoGame and Unity tie just behind for opposite reasons - MonoGame for zero-cost control (at the price of building tooling), Unity for turnkey console (at the price of money and trust). The matrix is a starting position: the POCs are what actually decide it.

Candidate stack combinations

Three coherent stacks, each internally consistent, aimed at different risk appetites.

rocket_launch

A · Godot-first (FOSS)

Godot 4 (C#) + Aseprite + LDtk/Tiled + Yarn Spinner + SQLite→JSON pipeline + Godot audio (FMOD later) + Git LFS + GitHub Actions; console via W4/porting house. Lowest cost, maximum control, best 2D. Console is the one paid dependency. Recommended

code

B · MonoGame (code-first)

MonoGame (C#) + Aseprite + Tiled + Ink/Yarn + SQLite + custom engine layer; console via registered dev + NDA SDKs. The Stardew/Celeste path: total control and stability, but we build the editor and tooling ourselves.

verified

C · Unity (turnkey console)

Unity 6 (C#) + Aseprite + Tiled + Yarn Spinner + ScriptableObjects/SQLite + official console pipelines. Fastest, most certain console route and biggest ecosystem - at the cost of licensing spend and vendor-trust risk.

Proposed stack

Subject to POC validation, the recommendation is Stack A - Godot-first: it best matches a cozy, 2D, data-driven, budget-conscious passion project, and keeps us free of royalties and vendor lock-in. Concretely:

Cost summary

The stack is overwhelmingly free. Real spend clusters in a few places:

payments

Console porting

The dominant cost. Godot's W4 Consoles runs ~US$2,000/yr (studios under US$300k revenue) up to ~US$10,000/yr above; GameMaker's Enterprise console tier is ~US$960/yr. Engaged only when a vertical slice is proven.

graphic_eq

Audio middleware (optional)

FMOD is free under indie thresholds; only becomes a cost if the game's revenue/budget crosses them or we need deep adaptive audio.

sell

Small one-offs & fees

Aseprite (~US$20), platform dev-account/store fees, optional SaaS localization. All minor next to time.

The real budget is time, not licenses

With a FOSS-first stack, money is a rounding error next to the engineering and content effort. That is exactly what we want for a passion project: spend people, not licenses - and keep optionality by avoiding royalty and lock-in traps.

Next steps - POCs that would settle this

  1. Isometric render + day/night POC

    Build the same small scene in Godot (and optionally MonoGame) to test the pixel pipeline, Y-sort, and a lighting cycle.

  2. Data pipeline slice

    Take a handful of real facts from SQLite → validated JSON → in-engine content, to prove the spine end-to-end.

  3. Dialogue + input-abstraction slice

    Wire Yarn Spinner through one conversation, driven by touch, gamepad, and keyboard, to de-risk cross-platform input.

  4. Console feasibility check

    Confirm current W4/porting terms and requirements before treating Godot's console path as settled.
