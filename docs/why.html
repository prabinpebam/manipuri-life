The Pitch · What We're Making & Why

What's the proposal

A small, relaxing 2D pixel-art RPG set in the historical Meitei kingdom of Manipur - quest- and relationship-led, gentle, and quietly educational. This is the single page that lays out what we are making, why it deserves to exist, and how anyone - artists, historians, developers, sponsors - can help build it.

TL;DR

We are making a cozy, historically-accurate life sim set in the Meitei kingdom of Manipur. For anyone, it is a calm escape and a window into a world that is at once utterly different and deeply familiar. For Manipuris, it is their own history and culture made playable - learned by living it, not memorising it. For the wider world, it is an introduction to a place too few people know. This page is the pitch, and an open door for artists, historians, developers, and sponsors.

In a sentence

A quiet, no-pressure life sim where you live an ordinary year in old Manipur - farming, cooking, festivals, and neighbours - and come to understand a real, almost-forgotten world simply by living inside it.

What we're making

A small, relaxing 2D pixel-art RPG set in the historical Meitei kingdom of Manipur. It is light on farming and economy; the heart of it is quests, missions, and goals pursued while building friendships and relationships with the characters around you. It is a journey of discovery paced by rewarding milestones - unhurried, with very little penalty for taking your time. Along the way you come to know the people, their culture, and what daily life was actually like in old Manipur.

Genre
Cozy, relaxing 2D pixel-art RPG - historically grounded in the Meitei kingdom of Manipur.
The feel
No rush, no fail-states to speak of. A calm journey of discovery rather than a test of skill.
The core loop
Quests, missions, and goals that deepen your bonds with the characters - light farming and economy sit in the background, not the foreground.
The reward
Rewarding milestones and the slow, earned appreciation of a whole community as you become part of it.
What you take away
An understanding of the people, their customs, and how life was lived here centuries ago.

People are welcome to join this project for their own reasons. The personal goal underneath it all is simple: to introduce Manipur to the world, and to help this generation connect with the history of their own place like they never have before. Why it should exist

Most games ask for your reflexes, your competitiveness, or your time. This one asks for your attention, and gives you calm back. Its reason to exist is simple and two-sided: it is a place to rest, and a place to learn - and the two are the same act.

A break that actually restores you

No timers screaming, no score to chase, no way to lose. Just a chance to step out of modern life and get in touch with how living felt in a remote place, centuries ago - slow, tactile, and seasonal. You plant, you cook, you visit, you rest. The reward is the doing.

A window into another world

You meet a new place, a new people, and a living culture - and you come away struck by how different a life can be, and yet how familiar. The same mornings, meals, small worries, and quiet joys, centuries and half a world away. Difference and kinship, at once.

Who it's for

The game works on three layers at once. The outermost is universal; the innermost is deeply personal. Each one is a real reason for someone to care.

The Manipuri player

A chance to get in touch with their own history and culture in a way books and classrooms rarely manage - not dates to memorise, but ordinary days to inhabit. Heritage you can hold, cook, wear, and walk through.

The curious world

Everyone who has never heard the word "Manipur." For them this is a first meeting - a doorway that, we hope, leaves them a little in love with a place they didn't know existed.

A note from the maker: Manipur is a relatively little-known corner of the world. I want this game to be its introduction - a small, warm, honest doorway that sends a player off knowing this place is real, specific, and worth their curiosity. That personal reason sits underneath everything else here.

What makes it different

This is not a farming sim wearing a costume. It is a playable ethnography: historical accuracy is a design constraint, not set dressing. Every object, meal, tool, custom, and conversation is meant to answer a real question about how these people actually lived. We studied why cozy games work - see the Benchmarking deep dives - and rebuilt that feeling on top of genuine Meitei life, governed by a research bible and a cultural-integrity practice.

What we refuse to make

  • A world that looks Manipuri but behaves like a generic farming game
  • Borrowed festivals, invented customs, decorative "exotic" flavour
  • Accuracy sacrificed the moment it is inconvenient

What we are making

  • A world that behaves Manipuri - daily rhythms, real crafts, real calendar
  • Festivals, food, dress, and language grounded in evidence
  • Accuracy as the default, with a tiny, deliberate budget for playability

Pitches for the people who could build this

This is an open project, and it needs more than one pair of hands. Below is the specific case for each kind of collaborator - what you would shape, and what the work offers you back. If you see yourself in any of these, the door is open.

Artists & illustrators

The whole feeling of the game lives in its look: a warm, hand-made valley that makes a player want to stay. You would help define a visual language that is unmistakably Manipuri rather than generically "Asian pastoral" - architecture, dress, tools, light, and a possible English typeface shaped to echo the Meetei Mayek script.

Where you'd make the difference
Art direction, environment and character art, UI, and typography that carry authenticity at a glance.
What's in it for you
A rare subject with almost no visual stereotypes to fight, a research bible to draw from, and credited work on a game with a genuine cultural purpose.

Historians & culture-bearers

Accuracy is the product, so your knowledge is not a footnote - it is the source material the entire game is built from. You would help decide what a day, a meal, a festival, and a household actually looked like, and keep the team honest when convenience tempts us to fudge.

Where you'd make the difference
Primary-source research, review of depicted customs and language, and adjudicating the hard "we don't actually know" questions.
What's in it for you
Scholarship that reaches players, not just shelves - a way to put rigorous history in front of a global audience who would never open the monograph.

Developers & engineers

Underneath the calm is a real systems problem: a cross-platform life sim whose content is driven by a database of historical facts. You would help turn research into playable systems - the daily loop, crafting chains, the calendar, and the pipeline that turns facts into content across PC, mobile, and console.

Where you'd make the difference
Engine and gameplay code, the fact-to-content pipeline, tooling, and a cross-platform strategy planned from day one.
What's in it for you
An unusually meaningful codebase, clear design docs to build against, and a project where the engineering directly serves the storytelling.

Writers & narrative designers

The game's warmth comes from its people. You would give the valley neighbours worth caring about, quests that teach without lecturing, and a soft, seasonal arc - folklore, oral tradition, and small human stories drawn from the real world of historical Manipur.

Where you'd make the difference
Characters, dialogue, quest design, and the narrative scaffolding under the cozy loop.
What's in it for you
A setting overflowing with untold stories, and a mandate to write with tenderness rather than spectacle.

Composers & sound designers

Half of "cozy" is what you hear. You would build the soundscape of the valley - instruments, ambience, and seasonal themes rooted in Manipuri musical tradition - the audio that tells a player, within seconds, that they are safe here and somewhere specific.

Where you'd make the difference
Original score, environmental audio, and the emotional pacing of quiet moments.
What's in it for you
A living musical tradition to explore and reinterpret, and a game built to give your work room to breathe.

Sponsors & funders

This is a cultural-preservation project shaped as a game people will actually want to play. Support gives an under-represented culture a global stage, and backs a team whose planning - this documentation site - is transparent and serious from the outset.

Where you'd make the difference
Funding the time and craft that authenticity demands; enabling research, art, and a proper cross-platform build.
What's in it for you
Association with a purpose-led project, meaningful cultural impact, and visible progress tracked in the open on the documentation backlog.

The Manipuri community & players

This game is about your home, so it should be made with you, not merely about you. Your memories, corrections, and stories keep it true - and your play and word of mouth are how it reaches the world.

Where you'd make the difference
Lived detail, language and spelling, sensitivity review, and carrying the finished game outward.
What's in it for you
Your culture, rendered with care and put in front of everyone - and a say in how it is shown.
