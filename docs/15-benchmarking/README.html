Domain 15

Benchmarking & References

What we can learn from the best cozy life-sims - Stardew Valley and the Japanese rural-life games - so we borrow proven fun without importing someone else's culture.

TL;DR

We study reference titles to steal mechanics and feel, never setting. Stardew Valley sets the bar for the cozy loop and approachability; Japanese Rural Life Adventure - an authenticity-first restoration sim - shows how to make daily rural life culturally specific and ritual-rich (and, through its community's critiques, what anti-cozy friction to avoid). Each benchmark page pulls apart what works, what to adapt for historical Manipur, and what to deliberately reject.

Purpose

Owns
Competitive analysis, reference libraries, and a ledger of borrowed-vs-rejected mechanics.
Feeds
Game Design (loops & systems) and Art & Audio (look & feel targets).
Priority
P1 - shapes design early, revisited often.

Borrow the verb, not the world

The trap is copying Stardew's festivals or Japanese Rural Life Adventure's restoration ritual wholesale. We take the underlying mechanic (a seasonal festival; a self-sufficiency craft chain) and rebuild its content entirely from the Knowledge Bible so it is genuinely Meitei.

How we read a benchmark

Every reference page is scored through the same twelve-dimension lens - from premise and loops to motivation, characters, narrative, and art - ending in an Emulate / Adapt / Reject verdict per dimension.

Emulate

Proven mechanics and feel that transfer well - the cozy loop, gentle onboarding, meaningful NPCs.

Adapt

Good ideas that must be re-skinned to Meitei reality - festivals, crops, tools, calendar.

Reject

Things that would break authenticity or tone - anachronisms, borrowed folklore, wrong economy.
