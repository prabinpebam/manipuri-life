Benchmarking · Method

The Benchmark Lens

A shared framework for taking apart a reference game - twelve dimensions that explain why it works, so we can reproduce the feeling with historical-Manipur content instead of copying the game.

TL;DR

We read every reference title through the same twelve lenses - from the fantasy it fulfils, through its nested loops, motivation model, characters, narrative scaling, and art. Each dimension ends with a verdict: Emulate, Adapt, or Reject. The goal is to understand the machine, not to clone its parts.

Why a framework at all

Without a shared lens, benchmarking becomes "I liked this bit." The twelve dimensions force us to explain the mechanism behind a good feeling - which is the only thing that transfers to a completely different setting.

The twelve dimensions

auto_awesome

1 · Premise & fantasy

The wish the game fulfils. What life does it let you live, and why is that appealing?

loop

The nested loops - moment, day, season, year, lifetime - and how they interlock.

The verbs: farming, fishing, foraging, combat, crafting, building, socialising.

How you grow - skills, tools, automation, unlocks - and how the ceiling stays high.

Why you keep playing: autonomy, competence, relatedness, variable reward, low stakes.

balance

The scarcity - time, energy, money, seasons - that makes every choice matter.

diversity_3

NPC design, social systems, gifting, romance, and the sense of a living town.

auto_stories

Story arc, ambient storytelling, and how content keeps opening up over time.

palette

9 · Art & audio

Visual style, palette, portraits, UI, music, and how they set mood and readability.

school

10 · Onboarding & access

How it teaches without a wall of tutorials, and who it lets in.

favorite

11 · Emotional design

Tone, comfort, catharsis - the feeling players describe when they talk about it.

fact_check

12 · Verdict for us

Per dimension: Emulate, Adapt, or Reject - with the Meitei re-authoring named.

Loops are nested, not single

The engine of a life-sim is a stack of loops on different clocks. A great one keeps several running at once, so there is always a reason to continue "one more day."

The verdict scale

Emulate

The mechanism transfers as-is - copy the structure, keep the feel.

Adapt

The idea is good but its content is culture-bound - keep the shape, refill from the Knowledge Bible.

Reject

It would break authenticity, tone, or the era - deliberately leave it behind.

How to use this page

  1. Score each reference against all twelve

    Every benchmark page follows this exact order so titles are comparable.

  2. Name the mechanism, then the verdict

    Say why it works before deciding to emulate, adapt, or reject it.

  3. Roll findings into the synthesis

    Cross-game patterns collect on Lessons for Our Game.
