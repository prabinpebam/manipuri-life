Idea Inbox

A home for raw, unfiltered thoughts - mechanics, moments, features, worries - captured the instant they occur, before we know where they belong. We triage them into the proper documentation later.

TL;DR

This is a scratchpad, not a spec. Drop any idea here quickly and move on; nothing on this page is a decision or a commitment. Periodically we triage each entry - promote it into the right domain doc (Game Design, Narrative, Tech, etc.), park it, or let it go. Entries are dated and tagged so they are easy to find and sort.

How this inbox works

The goal is to never lose a thought to the friction of finding the "right" page for it. Capture is cheap; sorting is a separate, later job. Keep each entry short and honest - half-formed is fine.

  1. Capture

    Add the idea as a new entry with today's date, a few tags, and a Raw status. Don't polish.

  2. Let it sit

    Ideas get better (or quietly die) with a little time. No pressure to act immediately.

  3. Triage

    In review, each entry is Promoted into a real doc, Parked for later, or Dropped - with a link to wherever it landed.

Unexpected gifts from friends - surprise generosity

Raw 2026-07-04 #systems #relationships #emotional-design #marketing

A relationship-driven generosity system: once you have built genuine friendships, the world occasionally gives back in ways you did not ask for and did not expect. The target emotion is unearned, genuine gratitude - the warmth of being helped by someone when you weren't even looking for help.

  • Surprise gifting. A friend who somehow knows you need a particular resource just shows up and gifts you some. Deliberately infrequent - rarity is what makes it land; if it happens often it becomes an expected drip and the gratitude evaporates.
  • Hidden sacrifice. Later, the gift can quietly cost the friend something - they gave up something they needed to help you. This can be a thread the player never discovers, or, if they do discover it, something they can act on (repay, reciprocate, make it right).
  • Vulnerability & asking for help. A complementary mechanic where the player can ask for help - leaning on the community. The hard design problem is doing this without creating an exploit: it must not become a free resource tap or a mechanic to farm. Needs friction, social cost, or scarcity so it stays meaningful.
  • Publicity angle. Emotional, humane systems like this could earn genuine word-of-mouth and press - the kind of "did you hear what this game does" moment that travels.

Open threads to resolve if promoted

How does the game know what you "need" without feeling scripted? What sets the frequency so it stays rare? How is the hidden-sacrifice thread surfaced (or deliberately hidden)? And above all - how does "ask for help" resist abuse? Likely homes: Game Design (systems) and Narrative (the sacrifice stories).
