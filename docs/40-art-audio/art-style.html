Art & Art Style

The visual target for the game — the look, palette, and mood we reconstruct for historical Manipur, assembled from references and moodboards as they come in.

TL;DR

We're defining a cozy-but-authentic art style: warm timber-and-brick villages, wet paddy and Loktak water, natural-dye costume, and a palette pulled from Manipuri land and textile. This page is a living board — drop references and moodboards into docs/assets/art-style/ and they slot in below.

Reconstruct the year, not the postcard

The look must match the chosen canonical year — e.g. Kangla as it stood then, without the 1804 Kangla Sha statues. Cozy warmth is welcome; anachronism is not. When a visual choice and history conflict, the Knowledge Bible wins.

See the first concept renders

We now have early look-development art — a Kangla gatehouse across a day/night lighting cycle and a village yumjao. See Art Style — Initial Explorations.

Style pillars

Warm & lived-in

Cozy light, worn wood, cooking smoke — a place people belong, not a museum diorama.

Period-true

Every building, tool, and garment traceable to the era; no imported or modern motifs.

Land-driven palette

Colours from paddy green, Loktak blue, terracotta, and natural-dye textile.

Readable at a glance

Clear silhouettes and staging so the cozy loop stays easy to parse.

Working palette

A starting maroon-and-earth palette, to be refined against real textile and landscape references.

Moodboards

Reference library
