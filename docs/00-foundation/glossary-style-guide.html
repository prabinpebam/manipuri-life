Glossary & Style Guide

One canonical spelling for every Meitei term, and a house style for all our writing - plus the framework for deciding both, so the whole project reads as one careful voice.

TL;DR

Two things live here: a glossary of Meiteilon terms with a single agreed spelling each, and an editorial style guide for how we write. The framework is a transliteration-and-authority rubric - native speakers first, then scholarship, then searchability - with a confidence flag and mandatory review for sacred terms. A starter glossary and style rules follow, all provisional until confirmed by Meiteilon speakers.

Draft for review Every spelling needs native-speaker sign-off

Meiteilon-to-Latin transliteration is lossy and contested. Everything below is a working choice to be confirmed by Meiteilon speakers and checked against Cultural Integrity. A word can be technically defensible yet carry the wrong tone.

The decision framework - choosing a canonical spelling

For each term we may meet several spellings. We pick one canonical form using this order of authority, and record why.

  1. 1 · Native speakers & culture-bearers

    Meiteilon speakers and the advisory circle outrank every other source. When they settle a spelling, that's the answer.

  2. 2 · Scholarly transliteration

    Where native guidance is pending, prefer consistent academic transliteration over ad-hoc web spellings.

  3. 3 · Established / common usage

    If a form is already widely recognised (e.g. "Manipur"), lean toward it for familiarity - unless the community prefers otherwise.

  4. 4 · Searchability & pronounceability

    As a tie-breaker only, favour the form a global player can say aloud and type into a search box.

  5. 5 · Flag & rate

    Record variants, mark sacred/sensitive terms for review, and tag each entry Confirmed / Working / Needs review.

Standing decisions to make once, then hold

A few spellings ripple everywhere and should be decided deliberately: Meitei vs Meetei, Manipur vs Kangleipak, Meiteilon vs Manipuri (language), and whether we use diacritics. Pick each once; then every page obeys.

Editorial style guide - draft

House style so every page and every line of game text reads as one careful, warm voice.

Voice & tone
Warm, precise, humble. Plain English over jargon. Confident about the game, careful about the culture.
Non-English terms
Italicise a Meitei term on first use and gloss it once; plain thereafter. Proper nouns (Kangla, Loktak, Yaoshang) are capitalised and not italicised.
Uncertainty
Never state a shaky claim as fact. Flag speculation and rate it with the confidence model; "we don't know" is an acceptable, honest answer.
Punctuation
Use hyphens, not em-dashes. Curly quotes off; keep it simple and consistent. (Yes, this very rule is one we hold ourselves to across the docs.)
Dates & the era
The canonical era is written 1850-1900; specific years as bare numerals. Prefer day-month-year in prose.
Naming things
Refer to the game and places by their agreed canonical names only (see Name Brainstorming for the title); no drifting nicknames.
Sensitive framing
Religion, colonial history, and living traditions are described with care and never as casual "exotic" flavour. When in doubt, route to Cultural Integrity.

Open questions

  • Meitei or Meetei? The single most rippling spelling decision - needs a native call.
  • Diacritics or plain Latin? Accuracy vs. approachability for a global audience.
  • How much glossing in-game? Enough to teach, not so much it lectures - where's the line?
  • Do we show Meitei Mayek script alongside transliteration in-game, and where?
