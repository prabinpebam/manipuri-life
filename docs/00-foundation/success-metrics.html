Success Metrics & Audience

Who this game is for, and how we will know it worked - a framework for defining and measuring "they lived it," not a scoreboard of downloads.

TL;DR

Three audiences - anyone who needs to exhale, the Manipuri player, and the curious world. Success isn't installs; it's calm delivered, a place understood, a culture honoured. This page gives the framework: a single North Star plus a small set of signals across experience, learning, reach, and cultural reception - each measurable, tied to the vision, and validated by playtest. We explicitly refuse vanity and retention-maxing metrics that fight the pillars. Draft, not locked.

Draft for review Pick the framework before the numbers

Exact targets ("X% recall," "Y wishlists") come later. What matters now is agreeing what counts as success and how we'd measure it - so we build toward the right thing instead of whatever is easy to count.

The decision framework - what makes a metric worth having

Before any metric goes on the board, it must pass four tests. This is how we avoid measuring the wrong things.

Tied to the vision
It measures a pillar or the core promise (rest + a real place learned) - not a proxy that merely correlates with attention.
Measurable
A concrete method exists - a playtest observation, an interview, a survey, an in-game signal, or a review - even if the method is qualitative.
Actionable
A bad reading would change a real decision. If nothing changes either way, it's a vanity number.
Honest
We pair leading signals (early, noisy) with lagging ones (slow, trustworthy), and we welcome qualitative evidence over precise-but-hollow figures.

Anti-metrics we will not chase

Daily-active-user treadmills, playtime-maximisation, engagement dark patterns, and streak pressure. They optimise attention at the expense of calm - the opposite of what we promised. A cozy game that maximises retention has failed on its own terms.

Who it's for

The audience is layered - universal on the outside, deeply personal at the core. Each layer has its own picture of success.

The North Star

Our one guiding measure

Players finish feeling they lived an ordinary year in old Manipur - rested, and knowing a real place they didn't know before. Every supporting signal below exists only to tell us whether this is coming true.

The measurement framework

Five dimensions, each with what we want and how we'd know. Adapted loosely from HEART (happiness, engagement, adoption, retention, task success) but re-pointed at a calm, educational game - engagement here means "absorbed," never "hooked."

DimensionWhat success looks likeHow we'd measure (leading / lagging)
Calm & experiencePlayers feel rested, not pressuredPlaytest mood check-ins; session sentiment; "how did that feel?" interviews (leading); reviews mentioning calm (lagging)
Immersion / "lived it"They can describe a day, a custom, a neighbourPost-play "tell me about your day in the game"; unprompted recall of specifics (leading)
LearningThey learned something true about ManipurLight before/after knowledge check; almanac/collection completion as an implicit learning signal (leading/lagging)
ReachIt travelled beyond a nicheWishlists, word-of-mouth, press/creator coverage, geography of players (lagging)
Cultural receptionManipuris feel honoured and see it as accurateAdvisory-circle sign-off; community sentiment; corrections offered (leading/lagging)

How we'll measure "they lived it"

The hardest and most important signal - understanding - is measured mostly by listening, not analytics. A sketch of the protocol:

  1. Play, then talk

    After a session, ask open questions: "tell me about your day," "who did you meet," "what surprised you." Rich, specific answers are the signal.

  2. Recall over quiz

    Measure what stuck through unprompted recall, not a test - a quiz would betray "understanding by living."

  3. The almanac as a quiet tracker

    Collections and the in-game almanac double as an implicit map of what a player has encountered and learned.

  4. Close the loop with the community

    Manipuri players and the advisory circle judge whether "lived it" also means "got it right."

Open questions

  • What is the single North Star metric we'd stake the project on - and can we phrase it as one number or one sentence?
  • How do we measure learning without a quiz that undercuts the "learn by living" pillar?
  • What's the minimum bar for "cultural reception" before we'd feel comfortable shipping?
  • Which signals do we instrument in-game (privacy-respectingly) vs. gather only in playtests?
