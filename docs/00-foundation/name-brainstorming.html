Name Brainstorming

A living workspace for the game's title. We collect candidates, score them against shared criteria, and narrow toward a shortlist - the name is a Foundation decision because it colours branding, tone, and the title treatment.

TL;DR

The title should feel authentically Meitei, be easy for a global player to say and search, and hint at cozy valley life rather than war. Candidates below are grouped by theme and scored 1–5 on five criteria. Front-runners so far: Kangleipak, Ingkhol, and Chakhao. Nothing is locked - add your own and re-score.

Meanings need native-speaker sign-off

Every gloss below is a working translation. Before any name is shortlisted for real, its meaning, spelling, and connotations must be confirmed by Meiteilon speakers and checked against Cultural Integrity. A word can be technically correct yet carry the wrong tone.

What makes a good title here

Meaning
Carries a real, on-theme sense (home, land, harvest, belonging) - not just an exotic sound.
Pronounceable
A non-Manipuri player can say it after hearing it once.
Distinctive
Doesn't collide with an existing game, and stands out in a store listing.
Searchable
Spellable from sound; returns us (not a place or unrelated topic) on first search.
Cultural fit
A Manipuri reader nods rather than winces; the word isn't sacred, political, or misused.

Candidates by theme

Place & land

castle

Kangleipak

The historical name of the Meitei kingdom. Grand, authentic, and instantly ties the game to the real polity of the era.

shortlist

landscape

Leipak

"Land / country." Short, warm, easy to say; reads as a homestead you tend and belong to.

water

Loktak

The great floating lake. Evocative and iconic - but names a place more than a way of life.

Home & hearth

cottage

Ingkhol

"Homestead / garden plot." The closest fit to the Stardew "farm you make your own" feeling. Cozy and personal.

shortlist

home

Yumjao

"The great house / home." Suggests building a household and a life over time.

holiday_village

Khunou

"Village." Communal, gentle; centres the neighbours and festivals as much as the farm.

Season, harvest & nature

rice_bowl

Chakhao

Manipur's famous black rice. Edible, cozy, uniquely local - and a natural hook for the farming loop.

shortlist

local_florist

Sanarei

The marigold. Soft, seasonal, festival-coloured; pretty on a title card.

calendar_month

Mera

A month/season of lights and harvest. Single syllable, atmospheric, calendar-tied.

Compound & evocative

auto_stories

Tales of Kangla

Anchors to the royal seat; "Tales of…" signals a cozy narrative sim to global players.

woman

Leipak Leima

"Lady of the land." Lyrical; nods to the strong role of women in Meitei valley life.

terrain

Chak & Ching

"Rice & hill" - the valley/plain and the hills, the two halves of the world. Playful, alliterative.
